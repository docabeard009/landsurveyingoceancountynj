Home/Survey Histories
A proprietary asset

Survey histories others don't have.

When respected New Jersey surveyors retire or close, we acquire their historical survey records — an archive that can resolve a boundary or title question faster, and at lower cost.

Ask about your property (609) 201-4717

A boundary is only as good as the evidence behind it. Old monuments, prior surveys and decades-old field notes are often the key to resolving a corner, settling a dispute, or answering a title question — and much of that record exists only on paper, in the files of surveyors who have since closed their doors.

Lakeland actively acquires the survey histories of reputable New Jersey surveyors who are no longer in business. That growing archive frequently lets us pick up a property's history where a previous surveyor left off, rather than starting from nothing — which can mean a faster, more economical, and better-supported result for you.

How the archive helps your project

  • Resolving boundaries where original monuments are lost or disturbed
  • Supporting title and easement questions with documented history
  • Reducing fieldwork and research time on properties we already hold records for
  • Strengthening a survey's defensibility with a deeper evidentiary record

Think we may have records for your property?

If your property is in Ocean County or anywhere in New Jersey, it's worth asking. Send us the address and we'll check whether our archive includes relevant survey histories.
